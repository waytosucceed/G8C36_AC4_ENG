Writing Task:

Write a detailed narrative that incorporates both simple future tense and future continuous tense. Your narrative should include the following elements:

  • Setting the Scene: Describe a significant event or scenario that will take place in the near future (e.g., a major project, a significant personal event, or a planned trip).
  • Simple Future Tense: Use simple future tense to outline specific actions that will happen as part of the scenario. For example, describe plans or decisions that are expected to be made.
  • Future Continuous Tense: Use future continuous tense to illustrate actions that will be in progress at specific future moments during the scenario. Emphasise the ongoing nature of these actions.
  • Integration: Ensure that the narrative seamlessly integrates both tenses, showing how different aspects of the scenario will unfold and overlap.
  • Length and Detail: Your narrative should be approximately 250-300 words. Include enough detail to provide a clear picture of the future scenario and the various activities involved.

Example Framework:

  • Introduction: Briefly introduce the future scenario.
  • Simple Future Tense Examples: Describe key future plans or actions (e.g., "We will start the project next Monday").
  • Future Continuous Tense Examples: Detail actions that will be ongoing during specific future times (e.g., "At this time next month, we will be working on the final presentation").
  • Conclusion: Summarise how the different aspects of the scenario will interact and the anticipated outcome.
